SECURITY AND RISK MANAGEMENT AUGUST 2022

Module Home

Welcome to the Security and Risk Management Module. Security and risk are increasingly being treated as inseparable as enterprises move away from traditional siloed views of business towards more integrated, enterprise solutions. In this module we will look at the various areas where security and risk overlap and interact with each other. We will review the various methods of risk assessment – looking at both qualitative and quantitative methods. We will discuss a number of more traditional risk models (such as STRIDE, DREAD and Attack Trees) as well as reviewing the increasing trend towards hybrid models. We will also review common security standards such as PCI-DSS and look at business continuity and disaster recovery solutions. Finally, we will discuss some of the key research topics and advances driving the fields of security and risk management.

Unit-1: An Introduction to Security and Risk Management

Welcome to Week 1 where we will review some common definitions of, and approaches to Security and Risk Management.

Knowledge:
1. Review a number of common definitions of Security and Risk Management.
2. Review some common Security and Risk Management Concepts.
3. Discuss the Risk Management Process.

Learning Outcome:
1. Discuss the various definitions of risk.
2. Explain how to assess, qualify and mitigate risks.
3. Describe various approaches to quantify and qualify risks.
4. List common security and risk standards and select the appropriate one(s) for a given situation.

Unit-3: Introduction to Threat Modelling and Management

This unit focuses on approaches to designing Cyber Security solutions. There are various methods and techniques available to develop security solutions. To be able to design effective solutions, it is important to be able to effectively identify the threats and vulnerabilities which will inform the best solution to adopt. We will be exploring these principles that underpin good security designs.

Knowledge:
1. Discuss the difference between threats and vulnerabilities.
2. Review a number of threat modelling techniques.
3. Discuss a number of guides and cookbooks to help with threat modelling.

Learning Outcome:
1. Describe a number of threat modelling techniques.
2. Advise which technique should be used in specific situations.
3. Discuss when techniques should be combined in a hybrid model.

Unit-7: An Introduction to the Concepts of Quantitative Risk Modelling

Welcome to Week 7 where we will review a selection of methods that can be used as part of quantitative risk modelling. These include probabilistic approaches such as Monte Carlo simulations and Bayes theorem-based methods, as well as multi-criteria decision analysis techniques such as TOPSIS, AHP and ANP.
Knowledge:
1. Discuss what is meant by quantitative risk modelling (QRM).
2. Review different approaches to QRM including Monte Carlo Simulations and Bayes theoretical models.
3. Discuss the principles and antipatterns for each approach.
Learning Outcome:
1. Utilise quantitative risk modelling techniques to produce risk models.
2. Select the most appropriate technique based on the real-world problem.
3. Critically evaluate the pros and cons of the approach selected.

Unit-9: Risk, Business Continuity and Disaster Recovery

Welcome to Week 9 where we will discuss two of the most important tools in the armoury of risk management and mitigation: namely business continuity and disaster recovery plans.

Knowledge:
